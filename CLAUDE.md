# Nomad — Project Brief

## Client

- **Name:** Nomad
- **Industry:** Urban discovery / gamified local experiences
- **Target audience:** People living in Madrid who want to get out and do something different — locals, expats, young professionals, anyone with curiosity and a competitive streak
- **Tone:** Fun, competitive, clean, modern, aesthetic — the city is the hero, not the app
- **Copy voice:** Cool local insider, third person when possible, spotlight Madrid and its experiences rather than the user; no hype, no direct address — let the city speak

## Goals

- **Primary CTA:** Start your streak
- **Secondary CTAs:** Connect with friends, browse experiences
- **Competitors / inspiration:** Duolingo (gamification energy, reward loops), Habitica (streak mechanics), Streaks (clean streak UI), Meetup (event discovery) — stand apart with a cleaner, more aesthetic, city-first feel
- **Launch date:** Functional prototype in 5 days (2026-04-28)

## Brand

- **Primary color:** #22C55E (vibrant green — progress, momentum)
- **Secondary colors:** #3B82F6 (blue — social, connection), #F97316 (orange — reward, energy), #FACC15 (yellow — achievement, highlight)
- **Background:** #FFFFFF

## Pages

**Marketing (pre-login):**
1. `/` — Landing page
2. `/how-it-works` — How Nomad works
3. `/experiences` — Preview of experiences available in Madrid

**Product (post-login):**
4. `/dashboard` — Profile and personal experience dashboard, streak display
5. `/social` — Leaderboard, friends' streaks, social comparison

## Style Direction

**Typography:** Bold geometric sans-serif for headings to convey energy and modernity — recommend **Space Grotesk** (headings) paired with **Inter... no** — pair with **DM Sans** (body) for a clean, friendly read. Both available on Google Fonts. Never more than two families.

**Color mood:** The palette reads like a game UI — green for progress and streaks, orange for rewards and urgency, blue for the social layer, yellow for achievements and highlights. White is the dominant base (~80% of any screen). The vibrant accent colors appear only on small, intentional elements: buttons, streak badges, icons, and reward moments — never flooding a background or appearing all four at once. When a color must cover a larger surface, use a deepened variant (e.g. #16A34A instead of #22C55E) to avoid visual fatigue. One accent color leads per section; the others support.

**Layout:** Clean and spacious on the marketing side — generous whitespace, bold typography, card-based experience previews. Product side is more dense and data-forward: streak counters, leaderboards, activity feeds — structured but never cluttered.

**Animation:** Playful but purposeful — streak animations, number counters, card reveals. Framer Motion entrance animations on scroll for marketing pages. Micro-interactions on interactive elements (streak badge pulse, leaderboard position change). Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

**Imagery:** Photography-led — real Madrid locations, street-level and atmospheric. Complement with bold typographic overlays and minimal iconography. No stock-photo genericness — Madrid should feel recognizable and specific.

**Design vision:** Nomad sits at the intersection of a travel guide and a competitive game. The marketing site should feel like opening a window onto Madrid — bold, alive, specific. The product side should feel like a sports app: streaks, rankings, momentum. The thread connecting both is restraint — the color palette is energetic but the layouts are clean, letting the experiences themselves be the spectacle. Think Duolingo's dopamine loop meets a Monocle city guide.
